Who We Are & Our Foundation

Manala Foundation is a not-for-profit, non-governmental organization founded in May 2022 and officially registered in 26th July 2024 by the Tanzanian Ministry of Community Development, Gender, Women, and Special Groups. The organization is committed to empowering individuals and communities through education, healthcare, and economic development initiatives. By fostering collaboration and innovation, we aim to address systemic challenges and create positive lasting change.

Our Core Identity

Vision

A thriving and equitable community where every individual enjoys access to **education, healthcare, and sustainable livelihoods**.

Mission

Committed to empowering individuals and communities through **education, healthcare, and economic development** initiatives, fostering collaboration and innovation.

Values

Guided by principles of **Transformation, Effectiveness, Excellence, Relationality, Empowerment, and Passion** in all our work.

Our Core Pillars of Development

Education

Focusing on **literacy, numeracy, and vocational skills training** to provide children and youth with the key to a better future.

Healthcare

Ensuring access to medical services, **health education, and preventive care** in rural communities to improve well-being.

Economic Development

Promoting **entrepreneurship, microfinance, and sustainable agriculture** for women and youth groups to foster job creation and income generation.

Environmental Sustainability

Dedicated to **conservation and resource management** through community education and practical activities like tree planting.

Our Impact and Key Projects

January 2024: Health Support Initiative

Health Insurance for Vulnerable Children (Kahama)

In January 2024, the Manala Foundation successfully implemented a health support initiative aimed at improving the well-being of vulnerable children in Kahama. The Foundation provided **comprehensive health insurance coverage to 60 orphans** residing at the Mwime Orphanage Centre. This intervention ensures that the children will have timely access to quality healthcare services, reducing the risks associated with untreated illnesses and medical emergencies.

This program strengthens the overall welfare of the orphans and supports the orphanage in meeting the needs of the children under its care, reflecting our dedication to lasting impact through health and community empowerment.

April 2024: Scholarship Support Program

Uplifting Students from Economically Disadvantaged Households

In April 2024, the Manala Foundation successfully implemented its scholarship support program. A total of **12 low-income households benefited**, ensuring their children could continue pursuing education without the burden of financial hardship. The scholarship specifically supported students enrolled at **Nyandekwa Secondary School** and **Isagehe Secondary School**.

The assistance covered essential school-related expenses, enabling the students to attend classes consistently and focus on their academic goals. This intervention reflects the Foundation’s ongoing commitment to promoting equal access to quality education and breaking the cycle of poverty through empowerment.
